Bicycles Infrastructure #

Bicycles are one of the most important forms of transportation, but not in America. Aside from bicycles being better for the environment, and being more space efficient, bicycle infrastructure increases safety for all road users, including people in cars.

Here in Reno, what little bicycle infrastructure there is consists mainly of buffered and unbuffered bike lanes, some separated bike lanes, and bike boulevards.

  • Buffered and unbuffered bike lanes are standard one-way bike lanes separated from cars by a buffer zone, in the case of buffered bike lanes, and a single line of white paint, in the case of unbuffered bike lanes. They are often built on the shoulder of a road.
  • Separated bike lanes and paths can be either one or two ways, they are separated from cars by physical barriers such as curbs or flex posts.
  • Bike boulevards are where roads are designed to share car and bike traffic.

Safety Concerns #

The bicycle infrastructure in Reno is, in most places, not very safe, and in a few places, extremely unsafe.

For example, McCarran Boulevard has an extremely dangerous bike lane. With parts of McCarran having speed limits of up to 50 mph, and cyclists - if there were any - separated from cars by a single line of white paint, itâ€™s not rocket science to figure out why people donâ€™t use that bike lane.

Another one of the potentially most dangerous things to a cyclist are cars turning right. When turning right, cars normally pass through bike lanes, which increases the chance of hitting cyclists.

Additionally, on-street parking is bad for cyclists as it creates more opportunities for conflict.

For cases where bike lanes are between travel lanes and parking, drivers must pass through the bike lane, potentially hitting cyclists, and for cases where parking is between the travel lane and the bike lane, parked cars can prevent drivers from seeing cyclists, potentially causing accidents.

Overall, most bicycle infrastructure in Reno, and America as a whole, is poorly designed and dangerous.

Car-centric Design #

The primary ways people choose their main mode of transportation is through considering how comfortably and efficiently that mode can get them to any destination. This point is why nearly everyone in America uses a car; cars can get someone from any place to any other place because America, over the past 80 years, has invested massively into car infrastructure. One can drive from the driveway of any house to the parking lot of any building.

On the contrary, America has not invested much into pedestrian or bicycle infrastructure, one cannot easily traverse a city on foot or on a bike. Getting between buildings is a challenge with the discontinuous network of sidewalks and bike lanes, and even if one reaches their destination, many, if not most, buildings do not have bike racks. The bike network simply doesnâ€™t exist in a usable state.

Reno’s Biggest Little Bike Network #

Currently, the City of Reno and RTC Washoe are working on the Biggest Little Bike Network, a project meant to make walking and biking more comfortable in downtown Reno.

While the project is a good thing, and while it will improve downtown, there are much more pressing issues concerning pedestrian and cyclist safety and activity than the project addresses.

Namely, the lack of destinations in downtown, how shady downtown looks, and the lack of network connections beyond downtown. Ultimately, the Biggest Little Bike Network will be an improvement to the existing infrastructure, but otherwise, it will just be another isolated section of the network.

The bicycle-related issues plaguing Reno are pretty similar to the issues plaguing most of the rest of America; in Reno, bike lanes are dangerous, are useless, are political statistics. Yet these issues can change, so talk to the leaders, talk to other people, and do your own research.
